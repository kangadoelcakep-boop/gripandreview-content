Unboxing and Physical Features

The LI MSK 1010 is marketed as a brushless cordless drill. The packaging claims a maximum torque of 80 Nm and an RPM of 3,500. The unit features a 13 mm chuck diameter and three operating modes: screw, drill, and hammer/impact. The torque setting reaches a maximum of 20. Physically, the unit utilizes rubber covering on the handle and the bottom of the battery pack. However, observations noted that the gear shifting mechanism had some loose spacing, and one button on the unit was found to be non-functional or fake.

Verification of Claimed Specifications

Testing revealed significant discrepancies between the claimed and actual specifications. The claimed maximum RPM of 3,500 was found to be highly exaggerated. Measured RPM results were 383 RPM on speed 1 and 1282 RPM on speed 2. The battery capacity was also misleading; while the box claimed 3 Ah and the unit sticker stated 2 Ah, a discharge test using a 6A load resulted in an actual capacity of only 1300 mAh. Furthermore, the wall charger, claimed to be 1A, was measured to deliver a maximum current of approximately 0.55 amps (based on 11.1W maximum wattage).

Drilling and Impact Performance

The drill demonstrated strong performance in wood testing. It easily handled a 20 mm fan nozzle bit and a 13 mm auger bit on speed 2. When tested with a larger 19 mm auger bit, the drill stopped on speed 2 but was capable of completing the task successfully when switched to speed 1. In concrete testing, the drill was able to penetrate the material using the impact mode on speed 2, achieving a depth of approximately one finger plus.
