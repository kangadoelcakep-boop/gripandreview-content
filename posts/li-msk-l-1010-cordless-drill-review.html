Review Limasaki (Li-MSK) L 1010 Brushless Cordless Drill

Updated: 2026-01-11T22:23:41.147589+07:00 | Source: Grip & Review Authority

Unboxing and Design

The drill is branded Limasaki (Li-MSK) model L 1010 and features a 13 mm chuck diameter, three modes (screw, drill, impact), and torque settings up to 20. The unit has rubberized parts, and the single lock mechanism was confirmed to be working.

Observations noted that the gear shifting mechanism has some loose spacing, and a non-functional 'fake button' was present on the unit.

Specification Discrepancies

The claimed maximum RPM is 3,500 RPM. Measured RPM was 383 RPM (Speed 1) and 1282 RPM (Speed 2), indicating a large discrepancy from the claim.

The box claimed a 3 Ah battery, but the unit came with two 2 Ah batteries using 2000 mAh cqth cells.

The charger is claimed to be 1 A, but measured maximum current capacity was approximately 0.55 A.

Battery Capacity Test

A full battery (20.6 V) was subjected to a discharge test using a 6-amp load. The final measured capacity of the 2 Ah battery was only 1300 mAh (1.3 Ah).

Performance Testing

The drill easily handled a 20 mm fan nozzle bit and a 13 mm auger bit on speed 2.

When attempting to use a 19 mm auger bit, the drill stopped on speed 2 but was capable of completing the task on speed 1.

In impact mode, the drill was capable of drilling concrete, reaching a depth of about one finger length before stopping.

Transparansi Editorial

Metodologi: The review includes detailed measurements of RPM, battery capacity, and charging current, revealing significant discrepancies between claimed and actual specifications. Performance tests were conducted using various drill bits on wood and concrete.
