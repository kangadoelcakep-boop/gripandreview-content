Unboxing and Initial Impressions

The Li MSK L 1010 is a brushless cordless drill featuring a 13 mm chuck diameter and three modes: screw, drill, and hammer/impact. The unit has 20 torque settings and comfortable rubberized grips. The logo is applied via a sticker. The package typically includes the drill unit, a charger, and either one or two 21V batteries, along with various drill bits and accessories.

Specifications vs. Tested Reality

The manufacturer claims several high specifications that were contradicted by testing. The claimed maximum RPM of 3,500 was tested at only 1,282 RPM on speed 2 (and 383 RPM on speed 1). The battery, claimed to be 3.0 Ah, was found to contain 2000 mAh cells and yielded a tested capacity of only 1,300 mAh during a 6-amp discharge test. Furthermore, the 1-amp charger was measured to deliver a maximum current of only 0.55 amps.

Drilling Performance and Power

Despite the misleading specs, the drill demonstrated good power for its class. It easily handled drilling wood using a 20 mm fan nozzle bit and a 13 mm auger bit on speed 2. However, when attempting to use a larger 19 mm auger bit on speed 2, the drill struggled, stopped, and exhibited creaking sounds, suggesting limitations in the gearbox under heavy load. The drill was also tested successfully in impact mode on concrete, though it stopped after reaching a depth of about one finger.

Transparansi Editorial

Metodologi: Performance, RPM, and battery capacity were measured using dedicated tools (DL 2450 watt for discharge test). Manufacturer specifications were found to be highly misleading.
