"The Limasaki L 1010 offers strong performance for its low price point, but buyers must be aware that its claimed specifications for RPM and battery capacity are highly inaccurate."

Claimed Specifications

The Limasaki L 1010 is marketed as a brushless cordless drill with a 13 mm chuck diameter. Manufacturer claims include a maximum torque of 80 Nm and a high speed of 3,500 RPM. The packaging also claimed a 3.0 Ah battery capacity, although the included batteries were rated 2 Ah.

Build and Features

The unit features three modes (screw, drill, impact/hammer) and 20 torque settings. The grip uses soft rubber. Physical inspection revealed some build quality issues, including loose spacing in the gear shifting mechanism and the presence of a non-functional decorative button. The battery pack uses four MOSFETs and has a rubberized base.

Objective Measurements

Objective testing revealed significant discrepancies from the claimed specifications. The maximum measured RPM on speed 2 was 1282 RPM, far below the claimed 3,500 RPM. The charger, claimed to be 1 Amp, measured a maximum output of only 0.55 Amps. Furthermore, the 2 Ah battery was tested to have a real capacity of only 1300 mAh (1.3 Ah) upon discharge.

Real-World Performance

In performance tests, the drill easily handled drilling a 20 mm fan nozzle and a 13 mm auger bit into wood on speed 2. However, when attempting to drill with a larger 19 mm auger bit, the drill failed or stopped on speed 2, requiring a switch to speed 1 to complete the task. The drill was capable of drilling concrete using the impact mode, although one test showed the unit stopping after achieving a depth of approximately one finger length.
